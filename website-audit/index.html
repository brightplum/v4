Is Your Current Web Agency Actually Protecting Your Investment?

Most organizations spend $50K-$200K on their Drupal or WordPress website, then hand it to a maintenance vendor who can't deliver ROI. Get a free strategic audit to see what you're missing.

Your website isn't just a technical asset—it's a revenue driver, a trust builder, and often your organization's front door.

But here's what most maintenance vendors won't tell you:

Keeping your site running isn't the same as making it work for you.

They'll update plugins. They'll patch security holes. They'll fix broken links.

What they won't do:

  • Test changes before deployment (so bugs slip through to production)
  • Optimize for search engines (so your content doesn't get found)
  • Improve usability (so visitors leave frustrated)
  • Make your CMS easier for your team to use (so publishing takes twice as long as it should)

The result? You're paying for maintenance, but your website is declining in performance, visibility, and results.

The Three Gaps Most Maintenance Vendors Leave

Gap 1: No Testing = Hidden Risks

Cheap maintenance vendors apply updates without automated testing. They don't know if they broke something until your team reports it—or worse, until your users find it first.

What you need: Automated testing that catches issues before they reach your live site.

Gap 2: No Strategy = No ROI

Your maintenance vendor keeps the lights on. But are they:

  • Tracking how visitors use your site?
  • Finding and fixing usability problems?
  • Improving your search rankings?
  • Making recommendations that drive results?

What you need: Strategic oversight that turns your website into an asset, not just an expense.

Gap 3: No CMS Optimization = Wasted Staff Time

Your marketing team shouldn't need a developer to publish a blog post. Your content editors shouldn't be fighting with your CMS every day.

What you need: A vendor who improves your authoring experience, not just fixes what breaks.
